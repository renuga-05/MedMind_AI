Three Important Insights from the Dataset

1. Age is an important factor in heart failure cases. Most patients in the dataset belong to the middle-aged and elderly groups, indicating that heart-related conditions are more common among older individuals.

2. Ejection fraction values vary significantly among patients. Lower ejection fraction values suggest reduced heart pumping efficiency and may be associated with a higher risk of severe heart conditions.

3. Serum creatinine levels show noticeable variation across patients. Elevated serum creatinine levels may indicate kidney function issues, which are often linked with complications in heart failure patients.
